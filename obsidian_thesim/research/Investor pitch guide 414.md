# Investor pitch guide 414

## What investors should understand

They need to believe:

1. the problem matters,
2. the approach is differentiated,
3. the company can become large.

## The right framing

- **Finance first**
- **Engine first**
- **Platform later**

Do not present this as five separate startups.
Present it as one engine with one wedge and multiple future surfaces.

## What to emphasize

- The engine is the moat.
- [[Self-similarity]] is the primitive.
- The company starts narrow and thinks massive.
- The roadmap is ambitious, but the execution path is disciplined.
- The next milestones are measurable: edge, calibration, and paying users.

## What to avoid

- "We can predict markets."
- "This is guaranteed alpha."
- drowning investors in method names too early
- claiming fake TAM, fake traction, or fake certainty

## Best founder tone

- calm
- sharp
- ambitious
- specific

## Default answer to "why now?"

"The tooling and research are finally good enough to build this seriously, but the category is still open. The market still lacks a system that combines structural analog retrieval, explainability, and calibrated uncertainty."

## Default answer to "why won’t foundation models crush you?"

"They are useful benchmarks and components, but they are not the same thing as a structurally grounded engine. Our moat is the combination of analogue retrieval, scoring, calibration, and platform reuse."
