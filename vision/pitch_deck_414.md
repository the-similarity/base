## Slide 2 — Problem

Current forecasting tools break in one of three ways:

1. They are **too shallow**
   - simple pattern matching
   - indicator soup
   - visually persuasive, structurally weak

2. They are **too black-box**
   - generic foundation models
   - weak explainability
   - hard to calibrate under non-stationarity

3. They are **too narrow**
   - built for one domain
   - not reusable across finance, events, synthetic data, and narrative

Suggested presenter line:
"The world has plenty of dashboards and plenty of black boxes. What it does not have is a structurally grounded engine for finding meaningful analogs, projecting futures honestly, and reusing that same primitive across multiple categories."

---

## Slide 3 — Solution

**The Similarity** is a structural matching and probabilistic projection engine.

What it does today:

- searches history for analogous subsequences
- scores them across multiple mathematical lenses
- builds forecast cones from what happened next
- supports strategy, explainability, and exploration layers

Core idea:

**Not just shape matching. Structural matching.**

Suggested presenter line:
"We do not ask only, 'does this look similar?' We ask whether it scales similarly, evolves similarly, behaves similarly across topology, spectra, and dynamics, and whether those analogs are actually predictive."
