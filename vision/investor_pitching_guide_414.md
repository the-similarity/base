# The Similarity — Investor Pitching Guide 414

## The goal of the pitch

Your job is not to explain every method.

Your job is to make investors believe three things:

1. the problem matters,
2. your approach is differentiated and credible,
3. this can become a large company, not just a clever tool.

## The framing to use

Use this structure every time:

1. **Problem**
   - existing forecasting tools are shallow, black-box, or narrow

2. **Insight**
   - self-similarity is a more general primitive than point forecasting

3. **Product**
   - engine finds structural analogs and projects forward with uncertainty

4. **Wedge**
   - finance first

5. **Expansion**
   - synthetic data
   - 3D latent exploration
   - world-event prediction
   - natural language -> time series

6. **Why us**
   - engine depth
   - research rigor
   - long-term platform vision

## What to emphasize

### 1. Start narrow, think massive

Say:

"We are focused on finance first because it is the best proving ground."

Do not say:

"We are doing five startups at once."

The correct feeling is:

- focused wedge
- large platform potential

### 2. Sell the primitive, not just the feature

Say:

"The engine is the moat."

Investors want to know why this is not just a feature that gets copied.
Your answer is that the core primitive compounds across multiple categories.

### 3. Use honest ambition

Say:

"We are ambitious about the platform, but disciplined about the wedge."

This makes you sound dangerous in a good way, not unfocused.

### 4. Sound calibrated

Strong founders do not sound like gamblers.
They sound like people who know what must be proven next.

Say:

"The next milestone is not hype. It is measurable proof: forecasting edge, calibration, and paying users."

## What to avoid

### 1. Do not oversell certainty

Avoid:

- "We can predict markets."
- "We know what happens next."
- "This is guaranteed alpha."

Use instead:

- "We improve decision-making under uncertainty."
- "We search for structurally meaningful analogs."
- "We care about calibrated uncertainty, not fake certainty."

### 2. Do not drown them in methods

Do not open with:

- DTW
- Koopman
- TDA
- wavelets
- transfer entropy

Those are supporting evidence, not the first punch.

Open with:

"We built an engine that finds when the present rhymes with the past."

Then, if the investor is technical, unpack the stack.

### 3. Do not pitch five pillars as equal right now

Finance is the business wedge.
The other pillars are proof of platform breadth.

Use:

"Finance first, platform later."

## The ideal investor reaction

You want them to think:

- this is a real technical moat
- the founder sees farther than the first product
- the wedge is focused enough to execute
- the vision is broad enough to matter

## Recommended live pitch flow

### Opening: first 60 seconds

"We’re building The Similarity, an engine that finds when the present rhymes with the past, scores those analogs structurally instead of visually, and projects what tends to happen next with honest uncertainty. We start in finance, where the data is dense and the willingness to pay is high. But the deeper thesis is bigger: self-similarity is the primitive, and once you build that engine, you can apply it to synthetic data, event prediction, and even natural language converted into time series."

### The next 2 minutes

Cover:

- why current solutions fail
- what is unique in your engine
- why finance is the right wedge
- why this can become a platform

### The close

"If we are right, this is not a niche charting tool. It is a new computational layer for forecasting across domains."

## How to answer common investor questions

### "Why now?"

"Time-series models are improving, but the market is still missing a system that combines structural analog retrieval, explainability, and calibrated uncertainty. The tooling and research are finally good enough to build this seriously, but the category is still open."

### "Why won’t foundation models crush you?"

"They are important benchmarks and useful components, but generic foundation models are not the same thing as a structurally grounded engine. Our moat is the combination of analogue retrieval, scoring, calibration, and the ability to extend the same primitive across multiple products."

### "Is this a feature or a company?"

"Finance alone can support a real company. But the deeper reason this is a company is that the same engine can power synthetic data, event forecasting, and cross-domain state exploration. The wedge is one product. The primitive is a platform."

### "What is the first thing you need to prove?"

"Commercially: a small set of high-value users who keep coming back. Technically: rigorous forecasting edge and honest calibration."

### "What are the biggest risks?"

"The biggest technical risk is proving that structural analogs add real predictive value. The biggest business risk is trying to go too broad too early. That is why we’re finance first."

## Founder posture

Be:

- calm
- sharp
- ambitious
- specific

Do not be:

- defensive
- over-explanatory
- mystical
- desperate to impress

The tone should be:

"I know exactly what this is, what it is not yet, and what we need to prove next."

## Suggested closing lines

Pick one:

- "We’re not trying to make charts prettier. We’re trying to build a new intelligence primitive."
- "Finance is the proving ground. The engine is the company."
- "If self-similarity is the primitive, then this becomes much bigger than forecasting."

## Before you pitch live

Customize these fields:

- round size
- runway
- use of funds
- traction metrics
- pilot users / design partners
- benchmark results

Never improvise numbers in the room.
If you do not know a number, say what you are measuring and why it matters.
