Slide 02 Problem

Current forecasting tools break in three ways.

Too shallow

Indicator soup, visual analogs, and chart folklore look persuasive but rarely capture deeper structure.

Too black-box

Generic models are hard to trust, hard to explain, and often poorly calibrated when the regime changes.

Too narrow

Most tools solve one surface problem instead of building a reusable primitive across markets and other domains.

Slide 03 Solution

Not just shape matching. Structural matching.

  1. Search history for analogous subsequences
  2. Score them across multiple mathematical lenses
  3. Project forward using what actually happened next
  4. Express outcomes as calibrated ranges instead of fake certainty

We do not ask only whether two patterns look alike. We ask whether they scale alike, evolve alike, and behave alike across dynamics, topology, and spectra.
