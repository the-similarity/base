# The Similarity — Vision 2035

**Core thesis:** Self-similarity is the primitive. Everything else is an application layer.

---

## Pillar 1: Finance (Near-term)

Become a prop trading firm / hedge fund. The engine is the moat.

- Use the self-similarity engine to find historical patterns that rhyme with the present
- Generate forward projections (forecast cones) based on matched patterns
- Build trading strategies on top of pattern matching + projection
- Hire quants over time to develop strategies using the engine

**Why us:** Nobody else has a 9-method tiered similarity pipeline purpose-built for time series pattern matching at this depth.

---

## Pillar 2: Synthetic Data

Generate synthetic future time series with self-similarity to the past. Sell as training data.

- The world is running out of high-quality data for training
- The engine already finds self-similar patterns and projects forward — synthetic generation is a natural extension
- Produce plausible, statistically faithful synthetic time series for ML training, backtesting, stress testing
- Target: financial institutions, ML teams, researchers

---

## Pillar 3: 3D Data Space

Embed datasets as vectors in 3D space. Find self-similarity and correlations across different datasets.

- Represent time series as vectors (embeddings)
- Find cross-domain patterns: how does weather correlate with retail? How does sentiment correlate with markets?
- Visual exploration of dataset relationships in 3D space
- Cross-domain pattern discovery at scale

---

## Pillar 4: World Event Prediction

Use self-similarity in prediction markets and historical patterns to predict major world events.

- Track prediction market graphs alongside historical event data
- "This configuration of graphs has appeared before → here's what followed"
- Pattern-match across geopolitical, economic, social indicators
- Surface early signals from cross-graph similarity

---

## Pillar 5: Natural Language → Time Series

Convert human narrative into time series charts.

- User says: "I had a really bad morning, then my friend texted me and it got better"
- Engine generates: chart starts low → rises
- Personal time series: daily narratives get logged into a continuous personal graph
- Opens up self-similarity analysis on human experience data
- Makes the engine accessible to non-technical users

Every pillar connects back to the same primitive: **find patterns that rhyme across time, domain, and scale.** The engine we're building today for finance is the same engine that powers synthetic data, cross-domain discovery, event prediction, and narrative-to-data conversion. Build the primitive right, and the applications follow.
